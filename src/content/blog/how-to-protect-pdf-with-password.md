Protecting sensitive information inside your documents is more important than ever. Whether you're dealing with financial records, legal contracts, or personal data, locking your PDF with a password ensures that only authorized individuals can access its contents. 

In this guide, we'll show you exactly how to add a password to any PDF file directly from your browser—no software installation required.

## Why Protect Your PDF Files?

Adding a password to a PDF does more than just prompt for a code when opening; it fully encrypts the contents of the file. This means:
*   **Data Security:** If your email is intercepted or your USB drive is lost, your encrypted files remain inaccessible.
*   **Compliance:** Many industries require password protection for transmitting sensitive client information.
*   **Privacy:** Keep personal documents safe from prying eyes on shared devices.

With UsePDF, securing your document takes only a few seconds. The best part? **We never upload your file to a server.** All encryption happens directly inside your web browser.

## How Secure is Browser-Based Encryption?

Very secure! UsePDF uses modern WebAssembly and JavaScript libraries (like `pdf-lib`) to handle the actual encryption. Because the processing is "Client-Side," your original unencrypted file never leaves your computer. This eliminates the risk of your sensitive data being intercepted during an upload or stored on a third-party server.

## Forgot Your Password?

If you ever need to remove the password and you still remember it, you can easily use our [Unlock PDF](/tools/unlock-pdf) tool. However, if you *forget* the password, the file cannot be recovered. Encryption is designed to be unbreakable, so make sure to store your password in a safe place!
