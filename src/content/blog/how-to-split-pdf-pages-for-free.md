## The Problem with Large PDF Documents

Large PDF files present several logistical challenges in the modern workplace:

1. **Email Attachment Limits:** Most email providers (like Gmail or Outlook) cap attachments at 25MB. If you scan a heavily illustrated document, it will easily exceed this limit.
2. **Confidentiality Risks:** If a 50-page HR document contains sensitive employee information on pages 10-20, you absolutely cannot send the entire document to an external contractor who only needs to read pages 1-5.
3. **Information Overload:** Sending someone a massive file and telling them to "look at page 89" creates friction. Extracting just page 89 and sending a single-page document is much more efficient.

To solve this, you need a reliable **PDF separator**.

## Types of PDF Splitting Operations

Depending on what you are trying to achieve, you might be looking for a specific type of "cut." Here is a breakdown of the terminology and how to achieve it.

### The "Burst" Operation
If you search for "pdf page splitter," you might be looking to take a 10-page document and turn it into ten separate 1-page documents. This is often called *bursting* a PDF. It is highly useful when you have scanned a stack of diverse invoices in one go and need to file them individually.

### The "Trimming" Operation (PDF Cutter)
A "PDF Cutter" usually refers to trimming the fat off a document. For example, if you download a free eBook, it might have 5 pages of promotional material at the front and 10 pages of index at the back. By extracting a range (e.g., Pages 6 to 150), you effectively "cut" off the unwanted ends. This pairs incredibly well with our [Delete PDF Pages tool](/tools/delete-pdf-pages) for surgical removals.

### The "Extraction"
If you only need a single chart or a specific table, you are performing an extraction. You tell the tool to grab Page 42, and you receive a fresh 1-page PDF.
